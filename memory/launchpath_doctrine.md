## WHAT LAUNCHPATH IS

LaunchPath is a compliance-first operating standard for new motor carriers.

- understand their exposure
- recognize how danger reaches the authority
- install the protection needed to guard against preventable collapse during the New Entrant period

**LaunchPath is NOT:**

- a packet dump

- hype

**LaunchPath IS:**
- a governed protection system

## CORE DOCTRINAL ORDER

```
REACH → Ground 0 → Four Pillars → AUTO → 16 Deadly Sins → Modules
```

---

## 1. REACH
**Role:** Exposure-awareness and readiness diagnostic.

REACH reveals:
- whether danger can already reach the authority
- how exposed the operator is right now
- how near danger already is
- how far that danger can reach in and do damage
- whether the operator should proceed, wait, or stop

**Locked definition:** REACH is the awareness and readiness layer. It reveals whether danger can already reach the authority, how exposed the operator is, and whether the operator should proceed, wait, or stop.

**Meaning:** REACH comes first because the carrier should know how exposed the operation is before moving forward.

---

## 2. GROUND 0
**Role:** Wisdom, posture, and consequence orientation.

Ground 0 is a free module. It is not the REACH test. It is not gated by a REACH GO result. It is available to any operator who wants to understand the system before building inside it. Ground 0 is the first briefing.

Ground 0 teaches:
- order before motion
- consequence before confidence
- stewardship before ambition
- seriousness before scaling
- the real cost of building without structure

**Locked definition:** Ground 0 is the wisdom and orientation layer. It trains the operator to think correctly about order, consequence, stewardship, and the real cost of building without structure.

**Meaning:** Ground 0 forms the operator's posture before the build begins.

---

## 3. THE FOUR PILLARS
**Role:** Protective guard structure around the authority.

The Four Pillars are the guard. They are the actual protective structure around the authority.

The pillars are:
- Authority Protection
- Insurance Continuity
- Compliance Backbone
- Cash-Flow Oxygen

**Locked definition:** The Four Pillars form the guard around the authority. They are the essential protective structure required to resist preventable breakdown and support stable operation.

**Meaning:** Not abstract values. They are the defensive structure that protects the authority where new carriers break most often.

---

## 4. AUTO
**Role:** Threat breach-path model.

AUTO is NOT the guard. AUTO explains the four ways threats try to get past the guard.

The four breach paths are:
- Around
- Under
- Through
- Over

**Locked definition:** AUTO is the breach-path model. It identifies the four ways failure attempts to bypass, penetrate, or overwhelm the guard around the authority: Around, Under, Through, or Over.

**Meaning:** AUTO explains how danger moves. It is the breach map, not the protection itself.

---

## 5. THE 16 DEADLY SINS
**Role:** Threat taxonomy.

The 16 Deadly Sins are the recurring preventable failures that most often expose, weaken, or damage a new carrier during the New Entrant period.

**Locked definition:** The 16 Deadly Sins are LaunchPath's threat taxonomy: the recurring preventable failures that most often expose, weaken, or damage a new carrier during the New Entrant period.

**Meaning:** These are the named threats the guard is built to resist.

---

## 6. MODULES
**Role:** Guard installation sequence.

The modules install the protection in practice:
- files
- records
- controls
- procedures
- rhythms
- operating discipline

**Locked definition:** The modules are the installation sequence. They build, reinforce, and maintain the protective structure required to guard the authority in practice.

**Meaning:** The modules do not merely explain the system. They install it.
