What FMCSA will actually look at first.

 

If you just got your authority, there's a window you need to understand before anything else.

FMCSA requires every new carrier to complete an 18-month new entrant period. During that window, a compliance investigator can show up — announced or unannounced — and audit your operation against seven safety categories.

What they will actually examine first:

— Driver qualification files — one missing item fails the category
— Drug and alcohol program enrollment — not a policy, an active program
— Hours of service records — the last 6 months, by driver
— Vehicle maintenance records — organized, current, accessible
 

Most carriers don't fail audits because they're running unsafe operations. They fail because their documentation doesn't match what they're actually doing. That's a fixable problem — but only before the investigator arrives.

Over the next two weeks, I'm going to walk you through the four failure patterns that account for the majority of new entrant revocations. Not theory. The actual patterns, and what closes them.
